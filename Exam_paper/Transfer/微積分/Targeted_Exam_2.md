**1.** (Targeted: Ratio Test) 
(a) Evaluate the limit $\displaystyle L = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \right|$ for the series $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{n!}{(2n)!} x^n$. 
(b) Based on the value of $L$, what can you conclude about the radius of convergence $R$ of the series? Explain your reasoning clearly.

**2.** (Targeted: Lagrange Multipliers)
Use Lagrange multipliers to find the absolute maximum and minimum values of the function $f(x, y) = x^2 y$ subject to the constraint $x^2 + y^2 = 3$. 
*(Hint: Be very careful with algebra when solving the system of equations $\nabla f = \lambda \nabla g$. Remember to consider cases where a variable might be zero before dividing, and substitute relations carefully.)*
