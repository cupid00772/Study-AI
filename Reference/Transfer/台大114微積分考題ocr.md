## 填空題 (Fill in the blanks. Each answer counts for 5 points.)

**1.** $\lim_{x\rightarrow0^{+}}\left(1-\frac{\sin x}{x}\right)^{\frac{1}{\ln x}}=\underline{(1)}.$

**2.** Let $f(x)=\int_{1}^{x-1}e^{-(t-1)^{2}}dt.$
(a) Find the Taylor series for $e^{-x^{2}}$ centered at $a=0$.
(b) Find the Taylor series for $f(x)$ centered at $a=2$.
(c) $\lim_{x\rightarrow2}\frac{f(x)-\arctan(x-2)}{(x-2)^{4}\ln(2x-3)}=\underline{(4)}.$

**3.** Suppose that near $(x,y)=(1,-1)$ the equation

$$
e^{x^{2}+y}-\ln\left(\frac{x}{y^{2}}\right)=1
$$

defines $y$ as a twice differentiable function of $x$ which is denoted by $y(x)$.
(a) The linearization of $y(x)$ at $x=1$ is $\underline{(5)}$
(b) $y^{\prime\prime}(1)=\underline{(6)}$

**4.** $\int_{0}^{2}\sqrt{\frac{4-x}{x}}dx=\underline{(7)}$

**5.** Suppose that $f(x,y,z)$ is differentiable near $(x,y,z)=(0,1,2)$ and $f(0,1,2)=10.$ Assume that curves

$$
r_{1}(t)=(3t, e^{2t}, 2\cos t)
$$

$$
r_{2}(t)=(\ln t, t^{2}, \frac{2}{t})
$$

lie on the level surface $f(x,y,z)=10$ and $f_{y}(0,1,2)=\frac{3}{2}$.
(a) The tangent plane to $f(x,y,z)=10$ at $(x,y,z)=(0,1,2)$ is $\underline{(8)}$.
(b) Let

$$
g(x,y,z)=
\begin{cases} 
\frac{|z-2|\sin(\pi x)}{|x|+2|y-1|+|z-2|}, & \text{for } (x,y,z)\ne(0,1,2) \\ 
0, & \text{for } (x,y,z)=(0,1,2) 
\end{cases}
$$

and

$$
u=\frac{\nabla f(0,1,2)}{|\nabla f(0,1,2)|}.
$$

Then $D_{u}g(0,1,2)=\underline{(9)}$.
(c) Assume that $f(x,y,z)$ attains the maximum value at $(x,y,z)=(0,1,2)$ when restricted to a level surface $h(x,y,z)=1$ where $h(x,y,z)$ is differentiable and $h_{x}(0,1,2)=6$. Using linear approximation, estimate the maximum value of $f(x,y,z)$ subject to the nearby level surface $h(x,y,z)=0.9$.
The estimated value is approximately $\underline{(10)}$.

## 計算與證明題 (Computation and Proofs)

**6.** Suppose that $f(x)$ is continuous on $[0,6]$ and $f(0)=f(4)=0$. The graph of $f^{\prime}(x)$ is given as below, but values of $f^{\prime}(1)$, $f^{\prime}(2)$ and $f^{\prime}(4)$ are not determined. It is known that

$$
\lim_{x\rightarrow4^{-}}f^{\prime}(x)=\infty \quad \text{and} \quad \lim_{x\rightarrow4^{+}}f^{\prime}(x)=-\infty
$$

*(Note: Please refer to the original exam paper for the graph of $y=f^{\prime}(x)$)*

(a) (5 pts) Find $\lim_{x\rightarrow2^{+}}\frac{f(x)-f(2)}{x-2}$ and $\lim_{x\rightarrow2^{-}}\frac{f(x)-f(2)}{x-2}$ by the Mean Value Theorem. Is $f(x)$ differentiable at $x=2$? Justify.
(b) (5 pts) List all critical numbers of $f(x)$ in the interval $(0,6)$. Where does $f(x)$ attain a local maximum? Where does $f(x)$ attain a local minimum?
(c) (8 pts) Find intervals on which $y=f(x)$ is concave upward. Find intervals on which $y=f(x)$ is concave downward. Find the inflection points of $f(x)$.
(d) (6 pts) Sketch the graph of $f(x)$ for $x\in[0,6]$.
(e) (6 pts) Suppose that

$$
f^{\prime}(x)=-\frac{32}{x^{2}+2x}-\frac{6}{x^{2}-6x+18},
$$

for $x>6$. Find $\lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}(f(x)-f(6))$.

**7.** Let $S$ be the part of the graph

$$
z=\cos x\sin y,
$$

$$
0\le y\le\pi,
$$

with upward orientation. Consider the vector field $F(x,y,z)=z\mathbf{i}+yz\mathbf{k}.$
(a) (10 pts) Parametrize the surface $S$ and evaluate $\iint_{S}\text{curl}\mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{S}$ directly.
(b) (10 pts) Let $C$ be the boundary curve of $S$. Decompose $C$ as the union of differentiable parametric curves and evaluate $\int_{C}\mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{r}$ directly. Show that the line integral equals the surface integral in part (a) which is consistent with Stokes' Theorem.
